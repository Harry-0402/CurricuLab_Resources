CASE STUDY 2

Testing Customer Engagement Drivers: Hypothesis Development at Netflix

1. Company Background (85 words)

Netflix is a global subscription-based streaming platform operating in over 190 countries. It generates revenue through tiered subscription models and invests heavily in original content production. Subscriber retention and engagement (watch time) are critical metrics for long-term profitability. In the Asia-Pacific region, Netflix has observed a plateau in average weekly watch hours per user despite content expansion.

2. Business Context (95 words)

Competition from regional streaming platforms has intensified. Marketing believes content localization improves engagement, while product teams argue that user interface personalization is more influential. The leadership team requires a structured research framework to test which factor significantly drives engagement growth.

3. Core Business Problem (100 words)

The company must identify whether localized content availability or algorithm-based personalization has a stronger impact on watch time. Management cannot simultaneously allocate high budgets to both initiatives without empirical justification. A well-defined hypothesis-driven research approach is required to avoid ambiguous findings and conflicting interpretations.

  • High Personalization Group: 11.2 hrs/week

5. Decision Situation (70 words)

Netflix must decide whether to prioritize:

  • Increasing localized content production, or

  • Investing further in AI-based recommendation algorithms.

Strategic capital allocation depends on robust hypothesis testing.

  1. How should research hypotheses be framed here?

CASE STUDY 3

Evaluating EV Adoption Trends: Applied vs Fundamental Research at Tesla

1. Company Background (90 words)

Tesla is a global electric vehicle manufacturer focusing on innovation, sustainability, and performance. It operates on a direct-to-consumer model and invests heavily in R&D and charging infrastructure expansion. As Tesla expands into emerging markets, understanding EV adoption behavior becomes strategically important.

2. Business Context (95 words)

In Southeast Asia, EV adoption remains below 5% of total vehicle sales. Governments are introducing subsidies and tax benefits. Tesla wants to understand whether consumer hesitation is driven by charging infrastructure concerns, price sensitivity, or performance perception. The research must guide immediate market entry strategy.

3. Core Business Problem (105 words)

Tesla must determine whether the study should focus on applied research (market entry decision support) or fundamental research (understanding long-term EV adoption behavior). Applied research would directly inform pricing and infrastructure investment decisions. Fundamental research would contribute to broader understanding of consumer behavior but may not yield immediate actionable insights. The company must align research objectives with strategic priorities.

5. Decision Situation (70 words)

Tesla must decide whether to:

  • Invest immediately in charging infrastructure partnerships,

  • Adjust pricing strategy, or

  • Conduct deeper behavioral research before entry.

6. Analytical Questions

  1. What distinguishes applied research from fundamental research here?

  2. How should the research problem be formally stated?

  3. What exploratory techniques may be useful?

  4. What risks arise from relying only on survey percentages?

  5. How should the research process proceed?

Q5: The research process should move from problem definition to exploratory research, hypothesis development, and structured quantitative validation.
