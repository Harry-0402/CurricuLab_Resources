CASE STUDY 1

Insurance: Life, Fire, Marine & General Insurance

Background

Prism Textiles Ltd. is a medium-sized manufacturing company operating in Gujarat. The company has taken:

  • A fire insurance policy for its factory premises
  • A marine insurance policy for export consignments
  • A group life insurance policy for employees
  • A general insurance policy for machinery and vehicles

In 2023, a major fire broke out in the factory due to an electrical short circuit, causing heavy damage to raw materials and finished goods. At the same time, one export consignment was damaged during sea transit due to improper packing. The insurer rejected part of the claims stating breach of policy conditions and non-disclosure of material facts.

The legal heirs of a deceased employee also filed a claim under the life insurance policy, which the insurer delayed citing incomplete documentation.

Questions

  1. Explain the nature and essential principles of insurance contracts.
  2. What is the significance of utmost good faith in insurance contracts?
  3. Distinguish between life insurance and general insurance.
  4. Examine the insurer's liability under fire insurance in this case.
  5. Discuss the scope of marine insurance for damaged export consignments.
  6. Can delay in settlement of life insurance claims be legally challenged?

CASE STUDY 2

Insolvency: Property, Debts & Discharge of Insolvent

Background

Rohan Enterprises, a sole proprietorship engaged in construction materials, suffered heavy financial losses due to market slowdown and non-payment by major clients. The business failed to repay bank loans, supplier dues, and statutory liabilities.

Creditors initiated insolvency proceedings against Rohan. During the process, it was discovered that:

  • Certain assets were transferred to relatives before insolvency
  • Some debts were unsecured
  • Rohan applied for discharge claiming inability to pay

The court must now decide on distribution of assets, validity of transfers, and eligibility for discharge.

Questions

  1. Explain the object and scope of insolvency law.
  2. What is meant by property of the insolvent?
  3. How are secured and unsecured debts treated in insolvency?
  4. Can pre-insolvency transfer of property be challenged?
  5. What conditions must be satisfied for discharge of an insolvent?
  6. What are the legal effects of discharge?

CASE STUDY 3

Carriage of Goods & Arbitration

Background

SkyBridge Electronics Ltd. transported goods:

  • By road from Pune to Mumbai
  • By sea to Singapore
  • By air for urgent deliveries

In multiple instances:

  • Goods were damaged during road transport
  • Sea cargo delivery was delayed
  • Air cargo was lost in transit

Contracts with carriers contained arbitration clauses. The company initiated arbitration proceedings instead of approaching courts. The carriers disputed liability, citing exceptions under carriage laws.

Questions

  1. Explain the rights and liabilities of carriers under carriage of goods by land.
  2. Discuss carrier liability under carriage of goods by sea.
  3. What is the legal position of carriers under air carriage?
  4. Explain the nature and advantages of arbitration.
  5. What are the modes of arbitration available?
  6. Can arbitration replace court proceedings in commercial disputes?
