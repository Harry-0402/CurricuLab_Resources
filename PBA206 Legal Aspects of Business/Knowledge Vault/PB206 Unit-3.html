Unit III – Company Law (Indian Companies Act, 1956)

 Introduction to Company Law

Concept: A company is created to solve the limitations of sole proprietorship and partnership (like limited capital and unlimited liability). It is a legal device used for large-scale business.

Definition:

"A company is a voluntary association of persons formed for a lawful purpose, having a separate legal existence, perpetual succession, and a common seal."

Key Features (Characteristics):

  1. Separate Legal Entity:The company is a distinct "legal person" separate from its members. It can own property and sue in its own name.
    • Note: Shareholders are owners, but they do not own the company's assets directly.
  2. Limited Liability: A shareholder’s liability is limited to the unpaid amount on their shares. Personal assets are safe.
  3. Perpetual Succession: Members may come and go (death, insanity, or sale of shares), but the company continues forever until dissolved by law.
  4. Common Seal: The official signature of the company (since it has no physical hands).
  5. Transferability of Shares: In a public company, shares are freely transferable.
  6.  Separation of Ownership and Management: Shareholders are owners, Directors manage affairs.

10. Prospectus

Meaning of Prospectus

Under the Companies Act, 1956, a prospectus means:

“Any document described or issued as a prospectus, including any notice, circular, advertisement or other document inviting deposits or offering shares or debentures to the public.”

üìå It is an invitation to offer, not an offer itself.

‚óè        Rule: It must contain full and true disclosures.

‚óè        Liability: Misstatements lead to Civil (Compensation) and Criminal (Jail/Fine) liability

13. Membership of a Company

Introduction: A member of a company is a person whose name is entered in the register of members. Membership gives legal rights and obligations.

Meaning of Member

A person becomes a member:

  2. By allotment or transfer of shares

üìå Shareholder and member are usually the same, but not always.

7. Winding Up (Dissolution)

The legal process of ending a company's life. Assets are sold, debts paid, and surplus distributed to members.

‚óè        Modes:
