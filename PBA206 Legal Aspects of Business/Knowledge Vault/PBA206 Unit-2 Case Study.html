CASE STUDY 1

Sale of Goods: Conditions, Warranties & Caveat Emptor

Background

Metro Electronics purchased 500 smart televisions from VisionTech Ltd. for resale during a festive season. The seller assured that the TVs were "latest models suitable for commercial resale." After delivery, it was found that:

  • Many units overheated after prolonged use
  • The model supplied was already discontinued
  • The defect was not visible at the time of purchase

Metro Electronics claimed replacement and compensation. VisionTech argued that the buyer should have inspected the goods before purchase and relied on the doctrine of caveat emptor.

Questions

  1. Distinguish between conditions and warranties under the Sale of Goods Act.
  2. Identify whether the seller breached a condition or a warranty in this case.
  3. Explain the doctrine of caveat emptor.
  4. What are the exceptions to caveat emptor applicable here?
  5. What remedies are available to Metro Electronics?

CASE STUDY 2

Transfer of Ownership & Performance of Contract of Sale

Background

Silverline Traders agreed to sell 1,000 bags of rice to FreshMart Ltd. The goods were identified but not weighed at the time of contract. Before delivery, a fire destroyed the warehouse where the rice was stored.

In another transaction, FreshMart delayed payment despite timely delivery of goods, while Silverline refused further deliveries under the contract.

Both parties dispute liability and risk.

Questions

  1. When does ownership in goods pass from seller to buyer?
  2. Who bears the risk of loss when ownership has not passed?
  3. Explain the relationship between risk and ownership.
  4. What are the duties of buyer and seller regarding performance of the contract?
  5. Can Silverline legally refuse further delivery? Give reasons.

CASE STUDY 3

Partnership: Formation, Rights, Liabilities & Dissolution

Background

Amit, Bhavesh, and Chirag started a logistics business under the name ABC Logistics based on an oral agreement. Profits were to be shared equally. No partnership deed was executed.

Later:

  • Bhavesh entered into a large contract without consulting others
  • Amit claimed extra salary for managing operations
  • The firm suffered losses due to Bhavesh's negligence
  • Chirag issued a public notice and retired from the firm
  • Creditors demanded payment from all partners.

Questions

  1. Explain the essential elements of a partnership.
  2. Is a written partnership deed compulsory? Explain.
  3. Discuss the implied authority of a partner.
  4. What are the rights of partners regarding remuneration and management?
  5. Explain the liability of partners for acts of other partners.
  6. What is the effect of retirement of a partner on firm liability?
  7. Under what circumstances can a partnership firm be dissolved?
