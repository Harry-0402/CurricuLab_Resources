Case Study: The Startup Dream – "Chai & Chill"

The Scenario Two college graduates, Arjun and Riya, decide to open a cafe called "Chai & Chill" in their hometown. During the process of setting up their business, they encounter several situations:

  1. The Furniture Deal (Offer & Acceptance): Arjun sees an advertisement by Mr. Wood, a carpenter, selling café tables for ‚Çπ5,000 each. Arjun visits Mr. Wood and says, "I will buy 10 tables for ‚Çπ45,000." Mr. Wood stays silent for two days, and Arjun assumes the deal is done.
  2. The Minor Mistake (Capacity): Riya needs a logo designer. She signs a formal contract with Sam, a brilliant 17-year-old graphic designer, agreeing to pay him ‚Çπ10,000 for a logo. After Sam delivers the logo, Riya realizes it’s not what she wanted and refuses to pay, citing that Sam is a minor.
  3. The Warehouse Threat (Free Consent): To store their supplies, they need a warehouse. The local landlord, Mr. Don, locks Arjun in a room and threatens to beat him up unless he signs a lease agreement for the warehouse at double the market rate. Fearing for his safety, Arjun signs it.
  4. The Coffee Machine (Breach & Remedies): They order a specialized espresso machine from "BrewTech Ltd." for ‚Çπ1 Lakh, to be delivered by opening day (June 1st). They pay an advance. BrewTech Ltd. sells the machine to another buyer for a higher price and fails to deliver to Arjun and Riya on June 1st. Consequently, "Chai & Chill" cannot serve coffee on their opening day and suffers a loss of reputation and revenue.
  5. The Insurance Policy (Contingent Contract): They sign a contract with "SafeGuard Insurance." The insurance company promises to pay ‚Çπ5 Lakhs if the cafe is damaged by fire within the next year.

Questions for Analysis

Based on the Indian Contract Act, 1872, answer the following:

  1. Is there a valid contract between Arjun and Mr. Wood regarding the tables?
  2. Can Sam (the 17-year-old) sue Riya for the ‚Çπ10,000 fee?
  3. Is the lease agreement signed by Arjun with Mr. Don valid? What can Arjun do?
  4. What legal action can Arjun and Riya take against BrewTech Ltd.?
  5. What type of contract is the insurance agreement?

Solutions & Legal Analysis

1. The Furniture Deal (Offer & Acceptance)

‚óè        Reasoning:

o        Arjun made a counter-offer ("I will buy for ‚Çπ45,000") which rejects the original offer.

o        For a contract to form, acceptance must be communicated.

o        Silence cannot be acceptance. Since Mr. Wood remained silent, he did not accept Arjun's offer. Therefore, no agreement exists.

2. The Minor Mistake (Capacity to Contract)

‚óè        Verdict: The contract is void ab initio (void from the beginning), but Sam might still get paid.

‚óè        Reasoning:

o        According to Section 11, a person must be of the age of majority (18+) to be competent to contract.

o        A contract with a minor is void ab initio. Therefore, the formal contract cannot be enforced in court.

o        However, under Quasi Contracts (Section 68), if "necessaries" or services are supplied to a minor or by a minor, they may claim reimbursement from the estate. But strictly speaking, as a contract, Riya cannot be sued for breach of contract because the contract itself was never valid in the eyes of the law.

3. The Warehouse Threat (Free Consent - Coercion)

‚óè        Verdict: The contract is Voidable at Arjun's option.

‚óè        Reasoning:

o        Consent must be free.

o        Mr. Don used Coercion (Section 15) by threatening to commit an act forbidden by the Indian Penal Code (threatening physical harm/wrongful confinement).

o        When consent is caused by coercion, the contract is voidable at the option of the aggrieved party (Arjun). Arjun can go to court and cancel the lease.

4. The Coffee Machine (Breach of Contract & Remedies)

‚óè        Verdict: BrewTech Ltd. committed an Actual Breach of Contract. Arjun & Riya can sue for Damages.

‚óè        Reasoning:

o        Failure to perform the obligation on the due date (June 1st) is an Actual Breach.

o        Remedies: Arjun and Riya can file a Suit for Damages.

o        They can claim Ordinary Damages (for the trouble) and potentially Special Damages (for loss of profit/reputation) if they had informed BrewTech that the machine was essential for the opening day.

5. The Insurance Policy (Contingent Contract)

‚óè        Verdict: This is a Valid Contingent Contract.

‚óè        Reasoning:

o        As per Section 31, a contingent contract is a contract to do something if a future uncertain event happens.

o        The event (fire) is collateral to the contract.

o        The contract is enforceable only if the event happens (i.e., if the fire actually occurs).
