CASE STUDY 2

Free Consent, Legality, Void & Contingent Contracts

Background

Sharma borrowed ‚Çπ5 lakh from Mehta under a written agreement that carried an interest rate of 48% per annum. Sharma agreed due to financial pressure. Later, Sharma discovered that Mehta had threatened to disclose personal information if the agreement was not signed.

In another agreement, Sharma promised to sell his house to Verma if he receives a foreign job offer within six months. Unfortunately, Sharma never received the offer.

Sharma now wants to know whether these contracts are legally binding.

Questions

  1. Explain the meaning of free consent under the Indian Contract Act.
  2. Identify whether Sharma's consent was free or not, and why.
  3. Discuss the legality of object and consideration in this case.
  4. Distinguish between void and voidable contracts.
  5. Is the agreement with Verma a contingent contract? Explain.
  6. What is the legal status of the contract if the contingency does not occur?

CASE STUDY 3

Performance, Discharge, Quasi Contract, Bailment, Agency & Remedies

Background

Amit gave his car to SpeedFix Garage for servicing. The garage entrusted the car to a trainee who damaged it during a test drive. Meanwhile, Amit mistakenly paid an electricity bill of his neighbor Rohit. Rohit refused to refund the amount.

In a separate transaction, Amit appointed Kunal as his agent to sell goods on commission. Kunal sold goods on credit against Amit's instructions and failed to recover payment.

Amit suffered losses and wants legal remedies.

Questions

  1. Explain the duties of a bailee in a contract of bailment.
  2. Is SpeedFix Garage liable for damage caused by its trainee?
  3. Discuss the concept of quasi contract with reference to mistaken payment.
  4. Explain the relationship between principal and agent.
  5. Has Kunal breached his duties as an agent?
  6. What remedies for breach of contract are available to Amit?
