1. Emerging Technologies

Introduction

In the context of digital transformation, emerging technologies are new or rapidly evolving technologies that have the potential to significantly change how businesses operate, compete, and create value. These technologies are not just tools; they reshape business models, customer expectations, operational processes, and industry structures.

Organizations that understand and adopt emerging technologies early often gain competitive advantage, while those that ignore them risk becoming obsolete. Unit 5 focuses on identifying these technologies and understanding why they are central to modern digital strategies.

  • Emerging Technologies

  • Technology Adoption Curve

  • Digital Disruption

  • Technology Convergence

  • Scalability

  • Automation

  • Data-driven decision-making

Detailed Explanation

1.1 What Are Emerging Technologies?

Emerging technologies are innovations that:

  • Are in the early or growth stage of development

  • Show high potential impact on business and society

  • Are not yet fully standardized or universally adopted

These technologies often evolve rapidly and improve over time through research, experimentation, and market adoption.

Several factors contribute to the rapid growth of emerging digital technologies:

3. Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Introduction

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the ability of machines and computer systems to simulate human intelligence. In digital transformation, AI plays a crucial role by enabling systems to think, learn, reason, and make decisions with minimal human intervention.

AI is no longer limited to research laboratories; it is now embedded in business operations, customer services, marketing, finance, healthcare, and manufacturing.

Key Concepts

  • Artificial Intelligence (AI)

  • Automation

  • Intelligent Systems

  • Natural Language Processing (NLP)

  • Computer Vision

  • Decision Support Systems

Detailed Explanation

3.1 What Is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence is a branch of computer science that focuses on creating systems capable of performing tasks that normally require human intelligence, such as:

  • Learning from experience

  • Understanding language

  • Recognizing patterns

  • Solving problems

  • Making decisions

AI systems use algorithms, data, and computing power to function effectively.

3.2 Types of Artificial Intelligence

Most business applications today rely on Narrow AI.

3.3 Core Components of AI

1. Data

AI systems require large volumes of structured and unstructured data.

2. Algorithms

Algorithms define how machines process data and make decisions.

3. Computing Power

High-performance processors and cloud platforms enable fast execution.

Cloud computing is a core enabler of digital transformation due to its cost-effectiveness and agility.
