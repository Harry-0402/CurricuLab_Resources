5. Single Location Decisions

Introduction

In a single location decision, the organization sets up only one facility to serve the entire market.

This is common when:

  • Production is centralized

  • Demand is concentrated

  • Economies of scale are important

Characteristics of Single Location Decisions

  1. Centralized Operations

    • All production/services from one location

  2. High Economies of Scale

    • Large-scale operations reduce cost per unit

  3. Simplified Control

    • Easier supervision and coordination

  4. Limited Flexibility

    • High dependency on one location

When Single Location is Preferred

  1. When raw materials are concentrated

  2. When technology is capital-intensive

  3. When market size is small or concentrated

  4. When highly skilled labour is available at one place

  5. When transportation cost is low

9. Facility Layout

Introduction

Facility layout refers to the physical arrangement of resources such as machines, equipment, workstations, storage areas, and service facilities within a plant or service organization.

A good layout ensures:

  • Smooth flow of work

  • Minimum cost

  • Maximum efficiency

Key Concepts

  • Material flow

  • Space utilization

  • Safety and comfort

  • Flexibility

Definition

Facility Layout is the physical arrangement of production facilities, including machines, equipment, workstations, and storage areas, in order to ensure smooth workflow at minimum cost.

10. Principles of Facility Layout

Introduction

The principles of facility layout act as guidelines to design an efficient and effective layout.

Major Principles

1. Principle of Integration

  • All factors such as:

    • Men

    • Machines

    • Materials

    • Methods
      should be integrated into a single coordinated system.

ðŸ“Œ Objective: Smooth and uninterrupted workflow.

2. Principle of Minimum Movement

  • Distance travelled by materials and workers should be minimum.

  • Reduces:

    • Material handling cost

    • Time

    • Fatigue

3. Principle of Smooth Flow

  • Materials should move in a straight line without backtracking.

  • Avoids congestion and delays.

4. Principle of Effective Space Utilization

  • Use both:

    • Horizontal space

    • Vertical space

  • Avoid overcrowding or wastage.

5. Principle of Safety and Comfort

  • Safe working conditions

  • Proper lighting and ventilation

  • Reduced accident risk

6. Principle of Flexibility

  • Layout should allow:

    • Future expansion

    • Changes in product or volume

7. Principle of Maximum Visibility

  • Easy supervision and control

  • Improves communication

11.1 Process Layout (Functional Layout)

Introduction

In process layout, machines performing similar operations are grouped together.

Characteristics

  • Machines arranged by function

  • Suitable for low-volume, high-variety production

  • Machine shop

  • Hospital departments (X-ray, surgery, lab)

11.2 Product Layout (Line Layout)

Introduction

In product layout, machines are arranged according to the sequence of operations.

Characteristics

  • Continuous flow

  • High-volume production

  • Standardized products

Example

  • Automobile assembly line

  • Electronics assembly

Advantages

  • Low material handling cost

  • Short production cycle

  • Easy supervision

Limitations

  • High initial investment

  • Breakdown affects entire line

Summary

Product layout is ideal for mass production.

11.3 Fixed Position Layout

Introduction

In fixed position layout, the product remains stationary, and resources move to it.

Characteristics

  • Product is large and heavy

  • Resources are brought to the site

Examples

  • Shipbuilding

  • Aircraft manufacturing

  • Construction projects

Advantages

  • No movement of bulky product

  • High flexibility

Limitations

  • Complex coordination

  • High cost

  • Low equipment utilization

Summary

Fixed position layout suits project-based production.

11.4 Combination (Hybrid) Layout

Introduction

Combination layout uses more than one type of layout in the same facility.

Examples

  • Automobile plant:

    • Process layout for parts manufacturing

    • Product layout for assembly

Advantages

  • Best use of advantages of different layouts

  • Greater flexibility

Limitations

  • Complex planning and control

Summary

Combination layout is most commonly used in modern industries.
